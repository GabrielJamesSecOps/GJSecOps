When Hackers Hold Your Code Hostage

In the startup world, speed often beats structure. But when ransomware hits, it can bring everything to a halt — fast.

What Is Ransomware?

Ransomware is a type of malware that encrypts your files and locks you out of your systems. Attackers demand a ransom — often in cryptocurrency — to give you the decryption key. It's extortion, plain and simple.

Why Startups Are Prime Targets

  • Minimal security budget: Most startups don't have dedicated security staff or tools in place.
  • Misconfigured cloud: Fast deployment often skips critical cloud hardening steps.
  • Weak backup strategies: Many startups rely on single-location backups, or none at all.

The Fallout of an Attack

  • Days to weeks of downtime
  • Loss of user trust and client data
  • Public reputation damage
  • Possible legal and compliance issues

How to Defend Against It

  • Backups: Regular, offsite, and ideally immutable backups are non-negotiable.
  • Endpoint protection: Use EDR solutions with behavioral detection capabilities.
  • Access control: Apply least privilege and strong MFA everywhere — including production infrastructure.
  • Incident planning: Run tabletop exercises so your team isn’t reacting to an attack for the first time during a crisis.

Bottom line: Prevention costs a fraction of what recovery does — and it's far less chaotic.
