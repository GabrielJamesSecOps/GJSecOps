The Real Cost of a Breach: Why Prevention is Always Cheaper

Think cybersecurity is too expensive? Try explaining a breach to your investors, customers, and legal team. The cost of cleanup — financial, reputational, and emotional — far outweighs the cost of basic prevention.

What Gets Hit When You Get Breached?

  • Revenue: Clients leave. New ones hesitate. Investors stall.
  • Reputation: Your brand becomes “the one that leaked data.”
  • Product velocity: Your engineers are pulled into damage control, audits, and forensics for weeks.
  • Legal risk: If you handle PII or financial data, lawsuits and fines follow.

What Does Cleanup Actually Cost?

  • 💸 $10K–50K on IR consultants and PR crisis management
  • ⏳ 1–3 weeks of frozen product development
  • 📉 Up to 30% churn in paying users (or worse)

How Much Would It Have Cost to Prevent?

  • ✅ $1K–3K/month for a basic SIEM and alerting
  • ✅ $2K for a one-time penetration test
  • ✅ $0 for well-written access control policies and secure code practices

Security Is an Investment, Not a Tax

You insure your office. You insure your laptops. You budget for customer support and payroll. Budgeting for security is no different — except that it protects every other thing you're building.

The best time to prepare for a breach is before it happens.
