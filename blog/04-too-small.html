The Startup Myth: “We’re Too Small to Get Hacked”

If I had a dollar for every time I heard this in a kickoff call, I’d be richer than most seed-funded CTOs. The truth? Hackers don’t care how small you are — they care how easy you are to exploit.

Why Small Companies Get Hit

  • Misconfigured clouds: Startups move fast and often skip hardening AWS, GCP, or Azure accounts.
  • Open ports: Exposed dev servers, staging environments, and admin dashboards often go unprotected.
  • No monitoring: No alerting = no visibility = easy pickings.
  • Juicy access: Even small companies use Stripe, Slack, GitHub, and AWS — all attractive attack targets.

Small Doesn’t Mean Invisible

Startups appear on open source dependency graphs, GitHub activity, press coverage, or even team members’ social profiles. Automated bots crawl all of this. You’re not hiding — you’re just unprepared.

Low Effort Defenses That Work

  • ✅ Lock down cloud permissions with IAM roles and least privilege
  • ✅ Enable 2FA/MFA on all accounts (and enforce it)
  • ✅ Patch fast — attackers scan for known CVEs within hours
  • ✅ Use read-only access for CI/CD and automation tokens

Being small doesn’t make you safe. It makes you soft — unless you secure smart.
