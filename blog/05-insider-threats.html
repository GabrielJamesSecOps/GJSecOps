Inside Job: Why Insider Threats Are Your Biggest Risk

Most companies worry about external hackers. But the most dangerous threats often come from the inside — people who already have access, already know the systems, and know exactly what’s valuable.

Types of Insider Threats

  • Disgruntled employees: People exiting the company who want to steal data, sabotage systems, or just vent their frustration.
  • Over-permissioned staff: Team members who can access more than they need — sometimes without knowing it.
  • Unintentional insiders: Well-meaning staff who fall for phishing, reuse passwords, or upload code to public repos.

Why Startups Are Vulnerable

  • No user access reviews or automated revocation
  • Shared admin credentials or stale service accounts
  • Rapid onboarding without formal training
  • Inconsistent offboarding procedures

Prevention Tactics That Actually Work

  • ✅ Automate de-provisioning when people leave
  • ✅ Use role-based access control (RBAC)
  • ✅ Monitor download behavior and permission changes
  • ✅ Audit internal logs — not just external ones

Trust is good. Verification is better. Monitoring is best. Don’t wait for a former engineer to leak your entire repo.
