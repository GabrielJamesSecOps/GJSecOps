The First 5 Security Hires You Actually Need

Security is often deprioritized in early startup hiring plans. But waiting until Series C to hire security is like waiting until your engine explodes to get an oil change.

Here’s Who You Actually Need (and When)

1. Security-minded DevOps (Day 1–Series A)

Your infra person should understand IAM, secrets management, and logging from the start. They don’t need the title “security engineer” — but they need the mindset.

2. GRC Consultant or vCISO (Seed–Series A)

Need help with SOC 2, ISO 27001, or working with enterprise clients? Hire someone fractional who’s done it before. Saves you months of risk down the line.

3. Security Engineer (Series A–B)

Your first full-time hire. They should own code scanning, secrets detection, internal tooling, and security automation. Look for someone who knows how to integrate with engineering — not block it.

4. Detection Engineer (Series B+)

Once you have logs, you need someone to make sense of them. This person builds alerts, refines signals, and reduces noise for the team — before alerts become alarms.

5. Governance Lead (Series C)

This role scales compliance, policy, and vendor risk — and takes that burden off your legal and product teams.

Security Isn’t One Role — It’s a Timeline

You don’t need a SOC team on Day 1. But you do need a plan. Build with security in mind early, and you’ll scale faster — and safer.
