Why Ethical Companies Still Get Breached

Just because your company has strong values doesn’t mean your systems are secure. Good intentions don’t protect data — good practices do.

The False Sense of Security

  • “We care about users” – but you don’t encrypt their data at rest.
  • “We believe in transparency” – but your security logs are off.
  • “We’re doing good in the world” – but your APIs are unauthenticated.

Hackers don’t care about your mission. They care about your access, your credentials, and your vulnerabilities. They’ll hit the nonprofit as fast as they’ll hit the hedge fund.

Real-World Examples

  • Environmental orgs getting breached via public S3 buckets
  • Healthcare startups leaking PII due to poor access controls
  • Education platforms attacked through vendor plugins

How to Align Values and Security

  • ✅ Make data privacy part of your culture and onboarding
  • ✅ Budget for security the way you budget for ethics reviews
  • ✅ Have a public disclosure policy (security.txt)
  • ✅ Treat user data like you would your own

Being ethical means being secure — because trust without protection is just a promise waiting to be broken.
